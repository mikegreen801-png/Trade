📊 Module 2: Chart Types
Candlestick Chart (BEST FOR DAY TRADING)
Each "candle" represents one time period (1 minute, 5 minutes, 1 hour, etc.)
🟩 GREEN (bullish) = Close > Open
🟥 RED (bearish) = Close < Open
📍 Wick = High & Low of period
Anatomy of a Candle:
┌─ HIGH (top of upper wick)
│
│ ┌─────────┐ ← CLOSE
│ │ BODY │
│ │ (green) │
│ └─────────┘ ← OPEN
│
└─ LOW (bottom of lower wick)

Green candles = Buyers in control
Red candles = Sellers in control
Long wicks = Rejection at that price

Other Chart Types (Less Important)
Line Chart: Just connects closing prices. Too simple for day trading.

Renko/Heiken-Ashi: Filtered versions of candles. Nice but not required.

Volume Bars: Shows buying/selling force. Use WITH candlesticks (not alone).
✓ Recommendation: Use CANDLESTICK charts. They show everything you need.
🕯️ Module 3: Candlestick Patterns
The 4 Patterns You NEED to Know
1. DOJI (Indecision)
Open ≈ Close | Long wicks on both sides
Meaning: Nobody in control. Next candle shows direction.

2. HAMMER (Bullish Reversal)
Small body at top | Long lower wick
Meaning: Rejection of lower prices. Buyers stepping in.

3. SHOOTING STAR (Bearish Reversal)
Small body at bottom | Long upper wick
Meaning: Rejection of higher prices. Sellers stepping in.

4. ENGULFING (Reversal)
Current candle fully covers previous candle
Meaning: Momentum change. Strong conviction.

⚠️ Important: These patterns ONLY matter at support/resistance levels or with volume confirmation. A hammer in the middle of a down-trend is just noise.
💡 For Day Trading: Ignore individual patterns. Focus on candlestick REJECTION at support/resistance. If price touches a support level and bounces with a hammer, THAT matters.
📈 Module 4: Technical Indicators
The ONLY 3 Indicators You Need
1. Moving Average (MA)
What it is: Average price over last N candles
Why you need it: Shows the trend direction
Setting: 20-period (good for day trading)
How to use: If price is ABOVE the MA, bias is bullish. Below = bearish.

2. Support & Resistance (Manual Drawing)
What it is: Price levels where buyers (support) or sellers (resistance) are strong
Why you need it: Shows where to enter and where to stop loss
Setting: Use the drawing tool to mark past lows (support) and highs (resistance)
How to use: ENTER when price breaks resistance. RISK when price breaks support.

3. Volume
What it is: Number of shares traded in each candle
Why you need it: Confirms if a move has conviction
Setting: Volume bars show at bottom of chart (usually on by default)
How to use: Breakouts on HIGH volume are more likely to hold. Breakouts on LOW volume fail often.

⚠️ Avoid These Traps:
❌ Don't add 10 indicators (they'll all say different things)
❌ Don't rely on RSI, Stochastic, MACD alone (lagging)
❌ Don't confuse "indicator signal" with "entry signal"

Your entries come from PRICE ACTION (breakouts, bounces, support/resistance), not from an indicator crossing over.

✅ Module 6: Pre-Trade Checklist
Your Daily Setup Checklist
Run this checklist EVERY time before entering a trade:
  • Is the stock trading above its 20-day moving average?
  • Have I identified at least 2 support/resistance levels?
  • Is my entry at a level (not randomly in the middle)?
  • Is my stop loss 1-2% below entry (tight)?
  • Is my target at least 2x my risk?
  • Do I see volume confirmation on the entry candle?
  • Am I risking <1% of my account on this trade?
  • Have I already taken 3-5 trades today? (Time to stop)
  • Am I in a good emotional state? (Not revenge trading)
  • Is it before 2 PM? (Not trading the last hour of the day)
If ANY Box is Unchecked...
DO NOT TRADE.
Wait for the next setup. Quality > Quantity. One great trade beats ten mediocre ones.
